Project Description
This project, sponsored by Jason Gumaer from the Energy Justice Lab, focuses on Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) of utility disconnection data. The goal is to uncover trends and insights that inform energy equity policies, particularly how utility disconnections vary across states, time periods, and utility types. The project emphasizes analyzing data related to pre-COVID, COVID, and post-COVID periods to understand the impacts of policies and interventions.

Recommendations for Future Teams
  1. Deep Dive into Policy Data
    Analyze state policies to understand significant changes in disconnection numbers. Focus on timing, enforcement, and scope of policies to uncover their impact.

  2. Update Analysis with New Data
    Re-run the analysis when post-2022/2023 data becomes available to track new trends and confirm previous findings.

  3. Comparative Analysis
    Compare disconnection patterns across states with similar demographics or regions (e.g., Eastern vs. Western US) but different policy frameworks to identify effective protections.

  4. Incorporating Additional Data
    Integrate external datasets (e.g., state population, income levels) to account for confounding factors and provide deeper insights into disconnection drivers.

  5. State-Specific Statistical Testing
    Expand the utility provider analysis to all states by tailoring period definitions based on state-specific disconnection trends. Use statistical tests to identify significant differences and patterns.
